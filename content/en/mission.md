---
title: Our Mission
description: Mission of the Leon Environmental Caucus
---

We want to slow or reverse climate change; increase the use of clean energy; protect air, water, and land from pollution; preserve natural resources including the Atlantic and Gulf coasts, lakes, rivers, wetlands, and forests; protect biodiversity; increase and protect publicly owned wilderness and parks and habitat for wildlife; make industry and agriculture sustainable; make cities healthy and livable places; and achieve environmental justice.

We support local and regional Democratic candidates whose positions reflect our environmental and Democratic values.

In 2017, we joined with ReThink Energy Florida to form what we then called Tally35 and now call Tally100 (Tallahassee 100% Together), a coalition to press for 100% clean renewable energy in Tallahassee and Leon County for all sectors, including electricity generation, transportation, and buildings. We urged the city to make this a goal for 2035, but city staff thought that too ambitious, and the commission set a goal of 2050 at the latest, with most city operations converted to clean energy by 2035.

We believe that immigrant justice is part of environmental justice. We seek to address the current and widespread harms caused by current ICE policies.

For the upcoming 2026 elections we are endorsing local candidates for Tallahassee City Commission and additional races throughout Leon County.

We support and coordinate with the campaigns of our endorsed candidates. We believe that electing environmentally-informed, strong democratic officials is key to our caucus mission.

We also co-sponsor the Big Bend Environmental Forum each election cycle.
